<TabItem label='Question'>
:::note[Difficulty: **Easy** - Category: **Structural Analysis**]
A simply supported beam span $L = 30~\text{ft}$ carries a uniform load $w = 2.0~\text{k/ft}$ (factored). Compute the maximum bending moment $M_{max}$ in **kip-ft**.
:::
</TabItem>

<TabItem label='Solution'>
:::tip[Solution]
Formula for maximum moment for uniform load:

$$
M_{max} = \frac{w L^2}{8}
$$

Substitute $w = 2.0~\text{k/ft}$ and $L = 30~\text{ft}$:

$$
M_{max} = \frac{2.0(30^2)}{8} = \frac{2.0(900)}{8} = \frac{1800}{8} = 225~\text{kip-ft}
$$

Thus **$M_{max} = 225~\text{kip-ft}$.**
:::
</TabItem>
