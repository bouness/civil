<TabItem label='Question'>
:::caution[Difficulty: **Medium** - Category: **Structural Analysis**]
A simply supported beam of span $L = 20~\text{ft}$ carries a uniform load $w = 0.6~\text{k/ft}$. The beam has $E = 29,000~\text{ksi}$ and $I = 300~\text{in}^4$. Determine the **maximum midspan deflection** in inches.
:::
</TabItem>

<TabItem label='Solution'>
:::tip[Solution]
**Formula for simply supported beam under uniform load:**

$$
\delta_{max} = \frac{5wL^4}{384EI}
$$

Convert units:

$$
w = 0.6~\text{k/ft} = 600~\text{lb/ft} = 50~\text{lb/in}
$$


$$
L = 20~\text{ft} = 240~\text{in}
$$


Substitute:

$$
\delta_{max} = \frac{5(50)(240)^4}{384(29\times10^6)(300)}
$$


$$
\delta_{max} = \frac{5(50)(3.32\times10^9)}{3.34\times10^{12}} = 0.25~\text{in}
$$

Thus, **maximum deflection = 0.25 in** at midspan.
:::
</TabItem>
