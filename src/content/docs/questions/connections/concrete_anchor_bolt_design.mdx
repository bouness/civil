<TabItem label='Question'>
:::danger[Difficulty: **Hard** - Category: **Connections**]
Four 3/4-in diameter cast-in anchor bolts are embedded 8 in into a concrete footing ($f'_c = 4000~\text{psi}$). The bolts are arranged in a 6x8 in pattern. Determine the factored tensile capacity per ACI 318 Appendix D. ($F_u = 58~\text{ksi}$)
:::
</TabItem>

<TabItem label='Solution'>
:::tip[Solution]
**Anchor Bolt Tensile Capacity**

**Step 1: Steel strength**
$A_{se} = 0.334~\text{in}^2$ (for 3/4" bolt)

$N_{sa} = A_{se} F_u = 0.334 \times 58 = 19.37~\text{kips/bolt}$

$\phi N_{sa} = 0.75 \times 19.37 = 14.53~\text{kips/bolt}$

**Step 2: Concrete breakout strength**
$h_{ef} = 8~\text{in}$

$N_b = k_c \lambda \sqrt{f'_c} h_{ef}^{1.5} = 24 \times 1.0 \times \sqrt{4000} \times (8)^{1.5}$
$N_b = 24 \times 63.25 \times 22.63 = 34,350~\text{lbs} = 34.35~\text{kips}$

**Step 3: Modification factors**
$A_{Nc} = (1.5h_{ef} + s_1)(1.5h_{ef} + s_2) = (12 + 6)(12 + 8) = 18 \times 20 = 360~\text{in}^2$

$A_{Nco} = 9h_{ef}^2 = 9 \times 64 = 576~\text{in}^2$

$\psi_{ed,N} = 1.0$ (edge distance > 1.5h_{ef})
$\psi_{c,N} = 1.0$ (cracking expected)

$\psi_{cp,N} = 1.0$ (for cast-in anchors)

**Step 4: Concrete breakout capacity**
$N_{cbg} = \frac{A_{Nc}}{A_{Nco}} \psi_{ed,N} \psi_{c,N} \psi_{cp,N} N_b = \frac{360}{576} \times 1.0 \times 1.0 \times 1.0 \times 34.35 = 21.47~\text{kips}$

$\phi N_{cbg} = 0.70 \times 21.47 = 15.03~\text{kips}$

**Step 5: Pullout strength**
For cast-in headed bolts: $N_{pn} = \psi_{c,P} 8 A_{brg} f'_c$

$A_{brg} \approx \frac{\pi}{4}(1.5^2 - 0.75^2) = 0.785(2.25 - 0.5625) = 1.325~\text{in}^2$

$N_{pn} = 1.0 \times 8 \times 1.325 \times 4 = 42.4~\text{kips}$

$\phi N_{pn} = 0.70 \times 42.4 = 29.7~\text{kips}$

**Step 6: Governing capacity**
The lowest capacity governs:
- Steel: 14.53 kips/bolt
- Concrete breakout: 15.03 kips total
- Pullout: 29.7 kips/bolt

**Factored tensile capacity: 14.5 kips per bolt**
