<TabItem label='Question'>
:::caution[Difficulty: **Medium** - Category: **Foundations**]
A square footing of size $B = 5~\text{ft}$ is placed at a depth $D_f = 3~\text{ft}$ in dense sand with unit weight $\gamma = 120~\text{pcf}$ and angle of internal friction $\phi = 36^\circ$. Using Terzaghi’s bearing capacity factors ($N_q = 41$, $N_\gamma = 45$), determine the **ultimate bearing capacity** $q_u$ and **allowable bearing pressure** assuming a factor of safety of 3.
:::
</TabItem>

<TabItem label='Solution'>
:::tip[Solution]
**Terzaghi’s bearing capacity for square footing:**

$$
q_u = 0.4\gamma B N_\gamma + \gamma D_f N_q
$$

Substitute values:

$$
q_u = 0.4(120)(5)(45) + (120)(3)(41)
$$


$$
q_u = 10,800 + 14,760 = 25,560~\text{psf}
$$


**Allowable bearing pressure:**

$$
q_{allow} = \frac{q_u}{FS} = \frac{25,560}{3} = 8,520~\text{psf}
$$

:::
</TabItem>
