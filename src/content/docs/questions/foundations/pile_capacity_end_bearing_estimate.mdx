<TabItem label='Question'>
:::danger[Difficulty: **Hard** - Category: **Foundations**]
An end-bearing driven concrete pile terminates on dense sand with an estimated end-bearing unit resistance $q_p = 80~\text{ksf}$. The pile tip area is $A_p = 1.2~\text{ft}^2$. Estimate the ultimate end-bearing capacity $Q_p$ (kips).
:::
</TabItem>

<TabItem label='Solution'>
:::tip[Solution]
**Compute ultimate end-bearing:**

$$
Q_p = q_p A_p
$$

Units: $q_p = 80~\text{kips/ft}^2$, $A_p = 1.2~\text{ft}^2$.

$$
Q_p = 80(1.2) = 96~\text{kips}
$$

Thus, **ultimate end-bearing capacity = 96 kips** (note: shaft friction not included).
:::
</TabItem>
