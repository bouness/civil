<TabItem label='Question'>
:::caution[Difficulty: **Medium** - Category: **Foundations**]
A 12-in concrete wall carries service loads: $D = 12~\text{kips/ft}$, $L = 8~\text{kips/ft}$. The allowable soil bearing pressure is 4000 psf. $f'_c = 3000~\text{psi}$, $f_y = 60000~\text{psi}$. Design the wall footing width and depth.
:::
</TabItem>

<TabItem label='Solution'>
:::tip[Solution]
**Wall Footing Design**

**Step 1: Determine footing width**
Total service load = $D + L = 12 + 8 = 20~\text{kips/ft}$

Assume 1-ft thick footing initially:
Footing weight = $1 \times 1 \times 0.150 = 0.150~\text{kips/ft}$

Total load on soil = $20 + 0.150 = 20.15~\text{kips/ft}$

Required width $B = \frac{20.15}{4.0} = 5.04~\text{ft}$

Use $B = 5~\text{ft} = 60~\text{in}$

**Step 2: Check factored loads for depth**
$w_u = 1.2D + 1.6L = 1.2 \times 12 + 1.6 \times 8 = 14.4 + 12.8 = 27.2~\text{kips/ft}$

Soil pressure $q_u = \frac{27.2}{5.0} = 5.44~\text{ksf}$

Critical section for moment at face of wall:
Cantilever length = $\frac{5.0 - 1.0}{2} = 2.0~\text{ft}$

$M_u = \frac{q_u \times (\text{cantilever})^2}{2} = \frac{5.44 \times (2.0)^2}{2} = \frac{5.44 \times 4}{2} = 10.88~\text{kip-ft/ft}$

**Step 3: Determine required depth**
Assume #5 bars, $d \approx t - 3~\text{in}$

Try $t = 12~\text{in}$, $d = 12 - 3 = 9~\text{in}$

$R_n = \frac{M_u}{\phi b d^2} = \frac{10.88 \times 12,000}{0.9 \times 12 \times (9)^2} = \frac{130,600}{874.8} = 149~\text{psi}$

$\rho = \frac{0.85f'_c}{f_y} [1 - \sqrt{1 - \frac{2R_n}{0.85f'_c}}] = \frac{0.85\times3}{60} [1 - \sqrt{1 - \frac{2\times149}{0.85\times3000}}]$

$\rho = 0.0425 [1 - \sqrt{1 - \frac{298}{2550}}] = 0.0425 [1 - \sqrt{1 - 0.1169}] = 0.0425 [1 - \sqrt{0.8831}] = 0.0425 [1 - 0.9397] = 0.00256$

Minimum $\rho = 0.0018$ ✓

$A_s = \rho b d = 0.00256 \times 12 \times 9 = 0.277~\text{in}^2/ft$

Use #5 @ 12 in ($A_s = 0.31~\text{in}^2/ft$)

**Step 4: Check one-way shear**
Critical section at $d$ from wall face:
Distance = $2.0 - \frac{9}{12} = 2.0 - 0.75 = 1.25~\text{ft}$

$V_u = 5.44 \times 1.25 = 6.8~\text{kips/ft}$

$\phi V_c = 0.75 \times 2 \sqrt{f'_c} b d = 0.75 \times 2 \sqrt{3000} \times 12 \times 9 / 1000 = 0.75 \times 2 \times 54.8 \times 108 / 1000 = 8.88~\text{kips/ft} > 6.8~\text{kips/ft}$ ✓

**Final design: 5-ft wide x 12-in deep wall footing with #5 @ 12 in**
