<TabItem label='Question'>
:::tip[Difficulty: **Conceptual** - Category: **Serviceability**]
Name two common control strategies designers use to reduce perceived floor vibrations in office buildings.
:::
</TabItem>

<TabItem label='Solution'>
:::tip[Solution]
Two common strategies:

1. **Increase natural frequency / stiffness** — stiffen beams/slabs or add structural elements so that the frequency moves away from the human-sensitive range (typically 1–10 Hz).  
2. **Increase damping / add tuned mass or dampers** — incorporate tuned mass dampers, viscous dampers, or increase floor finish damping to reduce amplitude.  
(Other strategies: increase mass, modify layout to reduce spans, add continuous soffit plates or diaphragms.)
:::
</TabItem>
