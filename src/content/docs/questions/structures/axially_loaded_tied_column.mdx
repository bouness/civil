<TabItem label='Question'>
:::caution[Difficulty: **Medium** - Category: **Structures**]
A short tied column has a 16 in Ã— 16 in cross-section with 8-#9 bars ($A_s = 8.0~\text{in}^2$). Material properties: $f'_c = 4000~\text{psi}$, $f_y = 60,000~\text{psi}$.

Determine the maximum factored axial load $P_u$ the column can support per ACI 318.
:::
</TabItem>

<TabItem label='Solution'>
:::tip[Solution]
**Step 1: Calculate gross concrete area**

$$
A_g = 16 \times 16 = 256~\text{in}^2
$$


**Step 2: Calculate nominal axial capacity**

For tied column:

$$
P_n = 0.80[0.85f'_c(A_g - A_s) + f_y A_s]
$$


$$
P_n = 0.80[0.85 \times 4 \times (256 - 8.0) + 60 \times 8.0]
$$


$$
P_n = 0.80[3.4 \times 248 + 480]
$$


$$
P_n = 0.80[843.2 + 480] = 0.80 \times 1323.2 = 1058.6~\text{kips}
$$


**Step 3: Apply strength reduction factor**

For compression-controlled members, $\phi = 0.65$ (tied column):

$$
\phi P_n = 0.65 \times 1058.6 = 688.1~\text{kips}
$$


**Answer:** Maximum factored axial load = **688 kips**
:::
</TabItem>
