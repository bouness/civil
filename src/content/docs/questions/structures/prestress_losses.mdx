<TabItem label='Question'>
:::danger[Difficulty: **Hard** - Category: **Structures**]
A **pretensioned concrete beam** has an initial prestress force of $300~\text{kips}$. Estimate the total prestress losses (in kips) due to elastic shortening, creep, shrinkage, and relaxation.
Assume the following loss values:
- Elastic shortening = $10~\text{ksi}$
- Creep loss = $15~\text{ksi}$
- Shrinkage loss = $8~\text{ksi}$
- Relaxation loss = $5~\text{ksi}$
The area of prestressing steel is $2.5~\text{in}^2$.
:::
</TabItem>

<TabItem label='Solution'>
:::tip[Solution]
**Total Prestress Losses**
Total loss in stress:

$$
\Delta f_{p,\text{total}} = 10 + 15 + 8 + 5 = 38~\text{ksi}
$$

Total loss in force:

$$
\Delta F_{p,\text{total}} = \Delta f_{p,\text{total}} \times A_{ps} = 38~\text{ksi} \times 2.5~\text{in}^2 = 95~\text{kips}
$$

The total prestress loss is $\mathbf{95~\text{kips}}$.
:::
</TabItem>
