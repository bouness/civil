<TabItem label='Question'>
:::caution[Difficulty: **Medium** - Category: **Structures**]
A simply-supported DF-L No. 1 glulam beam ($E = 1,600,000~\text{psi}$, $I = 10,000~\text{in}^4$) spans 24 ft and supports a uniform live load of $800~\text{plf}$ and a uniform dead load of $300~\text{plf}$.

Determine the live load deflection and check if it meets the common deflection limit of $L/360$.
:::
</TabItem>

<TabItem label='Solution'>
:::tip[Solution]
**Deflection Check**

**Step 1: Calculate Live Load Deflection**
The formula for maximum deflection for a uniformly loaded simple beam is:

$$
\Delta_{max} = \frac{5wL^4}{384EI}
$$


For live load, $w_L = 800~\text{plf} = 800/12 = 66.67~\text{lb/in}$.
$L = 24~\text{ft} = 288~\text{in}$
$E = 1,600,000~\text{psi}$
$I = 10,000~\text{in}^4$


$$
\Delta_L = \frac{5 \times 66.67 \times (288)^4}{384 \times 1,600,000 \times 10,000}
$$


First, calculate the numerator and denominator separately:
- Numerator: $5 \times 66.67 = 333.35$; $(288)^4 = 6.871\times10^9$; $333.35 \times 6.871\times10^9 = 2.29\times10^{12}$
- Denominator: $384 \times 1.6\times10^6 \times 1.0\times10^4 = 384 \times 1.6\times10^{10} = 6.144\times10^{12}$


$$
\Delta_L = \frac{2.29\times10^{12}}{6.144\times10^{12}} = 0.373~\text{in}
$$


**Step 2: Check Deflection Limit**
Common limit for live load: $L/360 = 288 / 360 = 0.80~\text{in}$.


$$
\Delta_L = 0.373~\text{in} < 0.80~\text{in}
$$


**The beam meets the $L/360$ live load deflection requirement.**
:::
