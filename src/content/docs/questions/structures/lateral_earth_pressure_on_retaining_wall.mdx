<TabItem label='Question'>
:::caution[Difficulty: **Medium** - Category: **Structures**]
A cantilever retaining wall retains 15 feet of granular backfill. The soil has a unit weight $\gamma = 120~\text{pcf}$ and an internal friction angle $\phi = 32°$. The backfill surface is horizontal.

Determine:
1. The active earth pressure coefficient $K_a$
2. The total active force per linear foot of wall
3. The location of the resultant force from the base
:::
</TabItem>

<TabItem label='Solution'>
:::tip[Solution]
**Step 1: Calculate active earth pressure coefficient**

Using Rankine's theory:

$$
K_a = \frac{1 - \sin\phi}{1 + \sin\phi} = \frac{1 - \sin(32°)}{1 + \sin(32°)} = \frac{1 - 0.530}{1 + 0.530} = \frac{0.470}{1.530} = 0.307
$$


**Step 2: Calculate total active force**

The active pressure varies linearly with depth:

$$
p_a = K_a \gamma H = 0.307 \times 120 \times 15 = 552.6~\text{psf}
$$


Total force per linear foot:

$$
P_a = \frac{1}{2}\gamma H^2 K_a = \frac{1}{2} \times 120 \times 15^2 \times 0.307 = 4144.5~\text{lb/ft}
$$


$$
P_a = 4.14~\text{kips/ft}
$$


**Step 3: Location of resultant force**

For triangular distribution, resultant acts at:

$$
\bar{y} = \frac{H}{3} = \frac{15}{3} = 5.0~\text{ft from base}
$$


**Answers:**
1. $K_a = 0.307$
2. $P_a = 4.14~\text{kips/ft}$
3. Location = **5.0 ft from base**
:::
</TabItem>
