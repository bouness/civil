<TabItem label='Question'>
:::caution[Difficulty: **Medium** - Category: **Structures**]
A **W12x65** steel column $A = 19.1~\text{in}^2$, $S_x = 87.9~\text{in}^3$ is subjected to an axial compressive load of $P = 200~\text{kips}$ and a bending moment about the strong axis of $M_x = 150~\text{kip-ft}$.
Determine:
The total maximum compressive stress in the column.
:::
</TabItem>

<TabItem label='Solution'>
:::tip[Solution]
**Total Maximum Compressive Stress**
The total stress is the sum of the axial and bending stresses.

$$
f_{\text{axial}} = \frac{P}{A} = \frac{200~\text{kips}}{19.1~\text{in}^2} = 10.47~\text{ksi}
$$


$$
f_{\text{bending}} = \frac{M_x}{S_x} = \frac{150~\text{kip-ft} \times 12~\text{in/ft}}{87.9~\text{in}^3} = \frac{1800}{87.9} = 20.48~\text{ksi}
$$


$$
f_{\text{total}} = f_{\text{axial}} + f_{\text{bending}} = 10.47~\text{ksi} + 20.48~\text{ksi} = 30.95~\text{ksi}
$$

The total maximum compressive stress is $31.0~\text{ksi}$.
:::
</TabItem>
