<TabItem label='Question'>
:::danger[Difficulty: **Hard** - Category: **Structures**]
A rectangular concrete beam with $b = 12~\text{in}$ and $h = 24~\text{in}$ is reinforced with 3-#8 bars in tension. The beam carries a service moment $M_s = 150~\text{kip-ft}$. Given: $f'_c = 4000~\text{psi}$, $f_y = 60,000~\text{psi}$, clear cover = 2 in, and $n = E_s/E_c = 8$.

Calculate the maximum crack width using the Gergely-Lutz formula: 
$$
w = 0.076 \beta f_s \sqrt[3]{d_c A}
$$
 where $\beta = h_2/h_1$ and $f_s$ is the steel stress.
:::
</TabItem>

<TabItem label='Solution'>
:::tip[Solution]
**Crack Width Calculation**

**Step 1: Determine steel stress $f_s$**
First, find the transformed section properties:

$A_s = 3 \times 0.79 = 2.37~\text{in}^2$
$nA_s = 8 \times 2.37 = 18.96~\text{in}^2$

Locate neutral axis by taking moments about the top fiber:

$$
\frac{1}{2} b x^2 = nA_s(d-x)
$$


$$
\frac{1}{2} \times 12 \times x^2 = 18.96(21.5 - x)
$$


$$
6x^2 = 407.64 - 18.96x
$$


$$
6x^2 + 18.96x - 407.64 = 0
$$

Solving: $x = 6.8~\text{in}$

Moment of inertia of cracked section:

$$
I_{cr} = \frac{1}{3} b x^3 + nA_s(d-x)^2
$$


$$
I_{cr} = \frac{1}{3} \times 12 \times (6.8)^3 + 18.96 \times (21.5-6.8)^2 = 1257 + 4088 = 5345~\text{in}^4
$$


Steel stress:

$$
f_s = n \frac{M_s(d-x)}{I_{cr}} = 8 \times \frac{150 \times 12 \times (21.5-6.8)}{5345} = 39.6~\text{ksi}
$$


**Step 2: Calculate parameters for Gergely-Lutz formula**

$d_c = 2 + 0.5 = 2.5~\text{in}$ (distance from extreme tension fiber to center of closest bar)

$A = \frac{2d_c b}{\text{number of bars}} = \frac{2 \times 2.5 \times 12}{3} = 20~\text{in}^2$

$h_1 =$ distance from neutral axis to centroid of reinforcement $= d - x = 21.5 - 6.8 = 14.7~\text{in}$

$h_2 =$ distance from neutral axis to extreme tension fiber $= h - x = 24 - 6.8 = 17.2~\text{in}$

$\beta = h_2/h_1 = 17.2/14.7 = 1.17$

**Step 3: Calculate crack width**

$$
w = 0.076 \beta f_s \sqrt[3]{d_c A} = 0.076 \times 1.17 \times 39.6 \times \sqrt[3]{2.5 \times 20}
$$


$$
w = 0.076 \times 1.17 \times 39.6 \times \sqrt[3]{50} = 0.076 \times 1.17 \times 39.6 \times 3.68 = 12.9~\text{mils}
$$


**Maximum crack width = 0.013 inches**
