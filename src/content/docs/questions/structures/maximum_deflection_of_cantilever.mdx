<TabItem label='Question'>
:::note[Difficulty: **Easy** - Category: **Structures**]
A **cantilever beam** of length $L = 15~\text{ft}$ supports a concentrated load $P = 5~\text{kips}$ at its free end. The beam is made of A992 steel $E = 29,000~\text{ksi}$ and has a moment of inertia $I = 400~\text{in}^4$.
Determine:
The maximum deflection of the beam.
:::
</TabItem>

<TabItem label='Solution'>
:::tip[Solution]
**Maximum Deflection**
For a cantilever beam with a concentrated load at the free end, the maximum deflection is given by:

$$
\Delta_{\text{max}} = \frac{PL^3}{3EI}
$$

Convert units to be consistent: $L = 15~\text{ft} \times 12~\text{in/ft} = 180~\text{in}$.
Substitute the given values:

$$
\Delta_{\text{max}} = \frac{(5~\text{kips}) \times (180~\text{in})^3}{3 \times (29,000~\text{ksi}) \times (400~\text{in}^4)}
$$


$$
\Delta_{\text{max}} = \frac{5 \times 5,832,000}{34,800,000}~\text{in} = \frac{29,160,000}{34,800,000}~\text{in} = 0.838~\text{in}
$$

The maximum deflection is $0.84~\text{inches}$ (downward).
:::
