<TabItem label='Question'>
:::caution[Difficulty: **Medium** - Category: **Structures**]
A **3/4-inch-diameter A325 bolt** in a double-shear connection passes through a **1/2-in thick gusset plate** and two **3/8-in thick web plates**. The bolt hole is a standard size. The connected material is A36 steel $F_u = 58~\text{ksi}$.
Calculate:
The nominal bearing strength of the bolt (in kips) based on the gusset plate.
:::
</TabItem>

<TabItem label='Solution'>
:::tip[Solution]
**Nominal Bearing Strength**
The nominal bearing strength per bolt is given by:

$$
R_n = 2.4 \times d \times t \times F_u
$$

Where:
$d = 0.75~\text{in}$
$t = 0.5~\text{in}$ (thickness of the gusset plate, the thinner part in this load direction)
$F_u = 58~\text{ksi}$
Substitute the values:

$$
R_n = 2.4 \times 0.75~\text{in} \times 0.5~\text{in} \times 58~\text{ksi} = 2.4 \times 0.375 \times 58 = 52.2~\text{kips}
$$

The nominal bearing strength is $52.2~\text{kips}$.
:::
</TabItem>
