<TabItem label='Question'>
:::danger[Difficulty: **Hard** - Category: **Structures**]
A **moment connection** must transfer a factored moment of $300~\text{kip-ft}$ and a factored shear of $80~\text{kips}$. The beam is a **W18x50** $d=18.0~\text{in}$, $b_f=7.5~\text{in}$, $t_f=0.57~\text{in}$, $t_w=0.36~\text{in}$.
Design:
The required number of **3/4-in A325 bolts** in the tension flange to resist the moment.
:::
</TabItem>

<TabItem label='Solution'>
:::tip[Solution]
**Required Number of Bolts**
The moment is resisted by a couple in the flanges. The tension force $T$ in the flange is:

$$
T = \frac{M}{d} \approx \frac{300~\text{kip-ft} \times 12~\text{in/ft}}{18.0~\text{in}} = \frac{3600}{18.0} = 200~\text{kips}
$$

A more precise formula uses $d - t_f$:

$$
T = \frac{M}{d - t_f} = \frac{3600~\text{kip-in}}{18.0 - 0.57~\text{in}} = \frac{3600}{17.43} = 206.5~\text{kips}
$$

The design strength of one **3/4-in A325 bolt** in single shear (threads included) is:

$$
A_b = \frac{\pi (0.75)^2}{4} = 0.4418~\text{in}^2
$$


$$
\phi F_{nv} A_b = 0.75 \times 54~\text{ksi} \times 0.4418~\text{in}^2 = 17.9~\text{kips per bolt}
$$

Number of bolts required:

$$
N = \frac{T}{\phi F_{nv} A_b} = \frac{206.5~\text{kips}}{17.9~\text{kips/bolt}} = 11.5~\text{bolts}
$$

Use $\mathbf{12~\text{bolts}}$ in the tension flange.
:::
</TabItem>
