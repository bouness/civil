<TabItem label='Question'>
:::caution[Difficulty: **Medium** - Category: **Structures**]
A W16x36 beam ($t_w = 0.295~\text{in}$) is connected to a column flange using four 3/4-in diameter A325-N bolts in standard holes. The bolts are placed at 3 in center-to-center spacing with 1.5 in edge distance. Determine the bearing strength of the beam web per AISC 360. ($F_u = 65~\text{ksi}$)
:::
</TabItem>

<TabItem label='Solution'>
:::tip[Solution]
**Bolt Bearing Strength Calculation**

**Step 1: Determine bearing strength criteria**
Per AISC 360 Section J3.10, the nominal bearing strength is:

For standard holes and deformation at service load not a consideration:

$$
R_n = 2.4dtF_u
$$
 (for each bolt)

**Step 2: Calculate bearing strength for interior bolts**
$d = 0.75~\text{in}$, $t = t_w = 0.295~\text{in}$, $F_u = 65~\text{ksi}$

For interior bolts (clear distance between holes > 1.5d):
Clear distance = $3.0 - 0.8125 = 2.1875~\text{in}$
$1.5d = 1.5 \times 0.75 = 1.125~\text{in}$
$2.1875 > 1.125$ âœ“

$R_n = 2.4 \times 0.75 \times 0.295 \times 65 = 34.5~\text{kips per bolt}$

**Step 3: Calculate bearing strength for edge bolts**
Clear distance to edge = $1.5 - 0.5 \times 0.8125 = 1.5 - 0.406 = 1.094~\text{in}$

Since $1.094 < 2.0d = 1.5~\text{in}$, use the edge bolt formula:

$$
R_n = 1.2l_ctF_u \leq 2.4dtF_u
$$


$$
R_n = 1.2 \times 1.094 \times 0.295 \times 65 = 25.2~\text{kips} \leq 34.5~\text{kips}
$$


**Step 4: Calculate total strength**
There are 2 edge bolts and 2 interior bolts:
Total $R_n = 2 \times 25.2 + 2 \times 34.5 = 50.4 + 69.0 = 119.4~\text{kips}$

**Step 5: Apply resistance factor**
$\phi = 0.75$ for bearing

$$
\phi R_n = 0.75 \times 119.4 = 89.6~\text{kips}
$$


**Design bearing strength: 89.6 kips**
