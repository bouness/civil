<TabItem label='Question'>
:::caution[Difficulty: **Medium** - Category: **Structures**]
A square spread footing must support a total service load of 250 kips (dead + live). The allowable soil bearing pressure is 4000 psf. The footing is 2 ft thick and weighs 150 pcf. Determine the required footing dimensions.
:::
</TabItem>

<TabItem label='Solution'>
:::tip[Solution]
**Footing Size Determination**

**Step 1: Calculate total load including footing weight**
Let $B$ = footing width (ft)
Footing area = $B^2~\text{ft}^2$
Footing volume = $B^2 \times 2~\text{ft}^3$
Footing weight = $B^2 \times 2 \times 150 = 300B^2~\text{lbs}$

Total load on soil = $250,000 + 300B^2~\text{lbs}$

**Step 2: Set up bearing pressure equation**
Bearing pressure = $\frac{\text{Total Load}}{\text{Area}} \leq 4000~\text{psf}$


$$
\frac{250,000 + 300B^2}{B^2} \leq 4000
$$


**Step 3: Solve for B**

$$
250,000 + 300B^2 \leq 4000B^2
$$


$$
250,000 \leq 3700B^2
$$


$$
B^2 \geq \frac{250,000}{3700} = 67.57
$$


$$
B \geq \sqrt{67.57} = 8.22~\text{ft}
$$


**Step 4: Select practical dimension**
Use $B = 8.25~\text{ft}$ (8 ft 3 in)

**Check:**
Footing weight = $300 \times (8.25)^2 = 300 \times 68.06 = 20,418~\text{lbs}$
Total load = $250,000 + 20,418 = 270,418~\text{lbs}$
Bearing pressure = $\frac{270,418}{68.06} = 3973~\text{psf} < 4000~\text{psf}$ ✓

**Required footing size: 8 ft 3 in × 8 ft 3 in**
:::
