:::caution[Difficulty: **Medium** - Category: **Structures**]
A double-shear bolted connection uses six 7/8-inch diameter A325-N bolts (threads not excluded from shear plane) to connect two plates. The connection transfers a factored load of $P_u = 180~\text{kips}$.

Determine if the connection is adequate for shear per AISC 360.
:::
</TabItem>

<TabItem label='Solution'>
:::tip[Solution]
**Step 1: Determine nominal shear strength per bolt**

For A325-N bolts (threads not excluded), $F_{nv} = 48~\text{ksi}$

Bolt area:

$$
A_b = \frac{\pi d^2}{4} = \frac{\pi (0.875)^2}{4} = 0.601~\text{in}^2
$$


Nominal shear strength per shear plane:

$$
r_n = F_{nv} A_b = 48 \times 0.601 = 28.8~\text{kips}
$$


**Step 2: Calculate total shear capacity**

For double shear, each bolt has 2 shear planes:

$$
R_n = n \times m \times r_n = 6 \times 2 \times 28.8 = 345.6~\text{kips}
$$


Where $n = 6$ bolts, $m = 2$ shear planes

**Step 3: Apply resistance factor**

$$
\phi R_n = 0.75 \times 345.6 = 259.2~\text{kips}
$$


**Step 4: Check adequacy**

$$
\frac{P_u}{\phi R_n} = \frac{180}{259.2} = 0.69 < 1.0
$$
