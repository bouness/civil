<TabItem label='Question'>
:::danger[Difficulty: **Hard** - Category: **Structures**]
A pretensioned concrete beam has a rectangular cross-section 12 in wide Ã— 24 in deep. The prestressing force after losses is $P_e = 180~\text{kips}$ located 6 inches below the centroid. The beam supports a superimposed dead load moment of $M_D = 150~\text{kip-ft}$ and live load moment of $M_L = 200~\text{kip-ft}$.

Given: $f'_c = 5000~\text{psi}$

Determine the stress at the bottom fiber under full service loads.
:::
</TabItem>

<TabItem label='Solution'>
:::tip[Solution]
**Step 1: Calculate section properties**

$$
A = 12 \times 24 = 288~\text{in}^2
$$


$$
I = \frac{12 \times 24^3}{12} = 13,824~\text{in}^4
$$


$$
c_b = 12~\text{in}
$$


Eccentricity: $e = 6~\text{in}$ (below centroid, positive for bottom fiber stress)

**Step 2: Calculate stress due to prestressing**

Axial component:

$$
f_1 = \frac{P_e}{A} = \frac{180,000}{288} = 625~\text{psi}
$$
 (compression)

Bending component at bottom fiber:

$$
f_2 = \frac{P_e \cdot e \cdot c_b}{I} = \frac{180,000 \times 6 \times 12}{13,824} = 938~\text{psi}
$$
 (compression)

**Step 3: Calculate stress due to applied loads**

Total moment:

$$
M = M_D + M_L = 150 + 200 = 350~\text{kip-ft} = 4200~\text{kip-in}
$$


Stress at bottom fiber (tension):

$$
f_3 = \frac{M \cdot c_b}{I} = \frac{4200 \times 12}{13,824} = 3,646~\text{psi}
$$
 (tension)

**Step 4: Calculate net stress**

Net stress at bottom fiber:

$$
f_{bottom} = -625 - 938 + 3646 = 2083~\text{psi}
$$
 (tension)

**Answer:** Stress at bottom fiber = **2083 psi tension**
:::
