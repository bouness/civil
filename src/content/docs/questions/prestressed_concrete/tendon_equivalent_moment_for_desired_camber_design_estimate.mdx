<TabItem label='Question'>
:::danger[Difficulty: **Hard** - Category: **Prestressed Concrete**]
You want an upward camber of approximately $0.25~\text{in}$ at midspan for a simply supported pretensioned beam of span $L = 40~\text{ft}$. Using the relation 
$$
\delta_{mid}=\dfrac{5 M L^2}{384 E_c I}
$$
 (solve for M) estimate the equivalent bending moment M (kip-ft) that the tendon eccentricity must produce. If typical tendon force per tendon after losses is about $50~\text{kips}$, estimate the approximate eccentricity e (in inches) required to create that moment (assume one equivalent tendon force for rough estimate).
:::
</TabItem>

<TabItem label='Solution'>
:::tip[Solution]
Rearrange to get the required moment:

$$
M = \frac{\delta_{mid} 384 E_c I}{5 L^2}.
$$

With $\delta_{mid}=0.25$ in, $L=40~\text{ft}=480$ in, $E_c=3.6\times10^6$ psi, $I=10{,}000~\text{in}^4$:
Computed equivalent moment: $M\approx 104.2~\text{kip·ft}$ (≈ 1.25×10^6 in·lb).
If one tendon carries $P=50$ kips (50,000 lb), eccentricity required:

$$
e\approx \frac{M_{in\cdot lb}}{P} \approx \frac{1.25\times10^6}{50{,}000} = 25.0~\text{in}.
$$

**Interpretation:** e≈25 in (this is a rough, illustrative estimate — in practice use distributed tendon profile or multiple tendons rather than a single large eccentricity).
:::
</TabItem>
