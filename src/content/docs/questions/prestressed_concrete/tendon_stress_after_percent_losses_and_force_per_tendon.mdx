<TabItem label='Question'>
:::caution[Difficulty: **Medium** - Category: **Prestressed Concrete**]
A tendon was tensioned to an initial steel stress $f_{pi} = 200~\text{ksi}$. Total losses are estimated as $12\%$. Tendon steel area is $A_p = 0.30~\text{in}^2$. Compute the tendon stress after losses (ksi) and the corresponding tendon force (kips).
:::
</TabItem>

<TabItem label='Solution'>
:::tip[Solution]
Loss amount:

$$
\Delta f = 0.12(200) = 24.0~\text{ksi}.
$$

Post-loss tendon stress:

$$
f_{p,service} = 200 - 24 = 176.0~\text{ksi}.
$$

Force per tendon:

$$
P = f_{p,service}A_p = 176.0\times0.30 = 52.8~\text{kips}.
$$

**Answer:** $f_{p,service}=176.0~\text{ksi}$, $P=52.8~\text{kips}$. 
:::
</TabItem>
