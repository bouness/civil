:::caution[Difficulty: **Medium** - Category: **Prestressed Concrete**]
A pretensioned beam has an equivalent constant upward prestress-induced moment $M = 30~\text{kip·ft}$ (apparent to the beam). For a span $L = 28.0~\text{ft}$, $E_c = 3.6\times10^6~\text{psi}$ and $I = 8000~\text{in}^4$, estimate the midspan camber (in inches) using the simplified relation 
$$
\delta_{mid}\approx\dfrac{5 M L^2}{384 E_c I}
$$
 (use consistent units).
:::
</TabItem>

<TabItem label='Solution'>
:::tip[Solution]
Convert units: $M = 30~\text{kip·ft} = 30\times12{,}000 = 360{,}000~\text{in·lb}$, $L = 28~\text{ft} = 336~\text{in}$.

Compute

$$
\delta_{mid}=\frac{5(360{,}000)(336)^2}{384(3.6\times10^6)(8{,}000)} \approx 0.0184~\text{in}.
$$

**Answer:** Midspan camber ≈ **0.018 in** (about 0.02 in).
:::
</TabItem>
