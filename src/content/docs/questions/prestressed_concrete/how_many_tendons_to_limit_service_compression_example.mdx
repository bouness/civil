<TabItem label='Question'>
:::caution[Difficulty: **Medium** - Category: **Prestressed Concrete**]
You wish to ensure the average service compressive stress from prestress does not exceed $3.0~\text{ksi}$ in a section having $A_g = 250~\text{in}^2$. Assume total losses are 10% and tendons are tensioned to $f_{pi}=200~\text{ksi}$ (i.e., post-loss stress per tendon $f_{ps}=180~\text{ksi}$). Each tendon area is $A_p=0.30~\text{in}^2$. Estimate the number of tendons required (round up to nearest whole tendon).
:::
</TabItem>

<TabItem label='Solution'>
:::tip[Solution]
Required total service compressive force:

$$
P_{service,req} = 3.0~\text{ksi} \times 250~\text{in}^2 = 750{,}000~\text{lb} = 750~\text{kips}.
$$

Post-loss force per tendon:

$$
P_{tendon} = f_{ps} A_p = 180~\text{ksi} \times 0.30~\text{in}^2 = 54.0~\text{kips}.
$$

Number of tendons:

$$
n = \frac{750}{54.0} = 13.89 \Rightarrow 14~\text{tendons (round up)}.
$$

**Answer:** Provide **14 tendons** (approx).
:::
</TabItem>
</Tabs>
