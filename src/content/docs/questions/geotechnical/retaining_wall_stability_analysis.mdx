<TabItem label='Question'>
:::danger[Difficulty: **Hard** - Category: **Geotechnical**]
A concrete cantilever retaining wall retains 15 ft of granular backfill with $\gamma = 120~\text{pcf}$, $\phi = 32°$. The wall has a 12 in thick stem, 8 ft wide base, and 18 in thick base. Concrete weighs 150 pcf. Check overturning and sliding stability with a level backfill. Assume the coefficient of friction between concrete and soil is 0.50.
:::
</TabItem>

<TabItem label='Solution'>
:::tip[Solution]
**Retaining Wall Stability Analysis**

**Step 1: Calculate active earth pressure**
$K_a = \tan^2(45° - \phi/2) = \tan^2(45° - 16°) = \tan^2(29°) = 0.307$

$P_a = \frac{1}{2}\gamma H^2 K_a = \frac{1}{2}(120)(15)^2(0.307) = 4144~\text{lbs/ft}$

Location: $\bar{y} = \frac{H}{3} = \frac{15}{3} = 5~\text{ft}$ from base

**Step 2: Calculate resisting forces and moments about toe**
Stem weight: $W_1 = 150(1)(15)(1) = 2250~\text{lbs}$
Moment arm: $x_1 = 8 - 0.5 = 7.5~\text{ft}$

Base weight: $W_2 = 150(8)(1.5)(1) = 1800~\text{lbs}$
Moment arm: $x_2 = 4~\text{ft}$

Soil on heel: $W_3 = 120(8 - 1)(15 - 1.5)(1) = 120(7)(13.5) = 11,340~\text{lbs}$
Moment arm: $x_3 = 1 + 3.5 = 4.5~\text{ft}$

Total vertical: $\Sigma V = 2250 + 1800 + 11,340 = 15,390~\text{lbs}$

**Step 3: Check overturning**
Overturning moment: $M_O = P_a \times \bar{y} = 4144 \times 5 = 20,720~\text{lb-ft}$

Resisting moment:
$M_R = 2250(7.5) + 1800(4) + 11,340(4.5)$
$M_R = 16,875 + 7200 + 51,030 = 75,105~\text{lb-ft}$

$FS_{OT} = \frac{M_R}{M_O} = \frac{75,105}{20,720} = 3.63 > 2.0$ ✓

**Step 4: Check sliding**
$F_s = \mu \Sigma V = 0.50(15,390) = 7695~\text{lbs}$

$FS_{slide} = \frac{F_s}{P_a} = \frac{7695}{4144} = 1.86 > 1.5$ ✓

**Wall is stable against overturning and sliding**
