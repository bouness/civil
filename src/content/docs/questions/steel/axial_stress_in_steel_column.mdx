<TabItem label='Question'>
:::note[Difficulty: **Easy** - Category: **Steel**]
A column transmits axial load $P = 150~\text{kips}$ and has gross cross-sectional area $A_g = 7.0~\text{in}^2$. Compute the average axial stress in ksi and check against $F_y = 50~\text{ksi}$ (is the stress less than $F_y$?).
:::
</TabItem>

<TabItem label='Solution'>
:::tip[Solution]
Convert load to pounds: $P = 150~\text{kips} = 150,000~\text{lb}$.
Axial stress:

$$
\sigma = \frac{P}{A_g} = \frac{150,000}{7.0} = 21,428.5714~\text{psi} = 21.43~\text{ksi}
$$

Compare to $F_y = 50~\text{ksi}$: $21.43 < 50$, so **stress is less than yield** (acceptable for gross stress check).
:::
</TabItem>
