<TabItem label='Question'>
:::danger[Difficulty: **Hard** - Category: **Steel**]
A simply supported steel wide-flange beam W12×50 supports a concrete slab that acts compositely (full shear connection). The plastic section modulus of the steel shape about strong axis is $Z_x = 200~\text{in}^3$. The slab contributes a steel-equivalent area such that the composite plastic section modulus increases to $Z_{comp} = 260~\text{in}^3$. With $F_y = 50~\text{ksi}$, compute the nominal plastic moment $M_p$ (in kip-ft) assuming full plasticity.
:::
</TabItem>

<TabItem label='Solution'>
:::tip[Solution]
**Compute plastic moment:**

$$
M_p = F_y Z_{comp}
$$

Substitute values (units careful):

$$
M_p = 50,000~\text{psi} \times 260~\text{in}^3 = 13,000,000~\text{in-lb}
$$

Convert to kip-ft: 1 kip·ft = 12,000 in·lb, so:

$$
M_p = \frac{13,000,000}{12,000} = 1,083.333...~\text{kip-ft}
$$

Round: **$M_p \approx 1,083.3~\text{kip-ft}$.**
:::
</TabItem>
