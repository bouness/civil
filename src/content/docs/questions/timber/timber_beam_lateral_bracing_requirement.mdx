<TabItem label='Question'>
:::caution[Difficulty: **Medium** - Category: **Timber**]
A 4×12 Douglas Fir beam spans 18 ft with full bearing at supports. The beam depth is 11.25 in and width is 3.5 in. Per NDS, lateral bracing is not required when $\ell_u/d \leq 7$. The unbraced length is 9 ft. Does the unbraced length obviate the necessity for lateral bracing?
:::
</TabItem>

<TabItem label='Solution'>
:::tip[Solution]
**Timber Beam Lateral Stability Analysis**

**Step 1: Understanding the double-negative**
'Obviate the necessity' means to **eliminate the need for** something.

This is a DOUBLE CONCEPT:
- 'Obviate' = eliminate
- 'Necessity' = requirement

So: Does the unbraced length eliminate the need for bracing?

**Step 2: Calculate slenderness ratio**
$\frac{\ell_u}{d} = \frac{9 \times 12}{11.25} = \frac{108}{11.25} = 9.6$

**Step 3: Compare to NDS limit**
Bracing not required when: $\frac{\ell_u}{d} \leq 7$

Actual ratio: $\frac{\ell_u}{d} = 9.6$

$9.6 > 7$

**Step 4: Determine bracing requirement**
Since $9.6 > 7$, the beam **EXCEEDS** the limit where bracing can be omitted.

Therefore, lateral bracing **IS REQUIRED**.

**Step 5: Answer the question (carefully!)**
Does the unbraced length obviate (eliminate) the necessity (need) for bracing?

**NO** - The unbraced length does NOT obviate the necessity for lateral bracing.

The beam ratio exceeds the limit, so bracing **CANNOT** be eliminated. Bracing is necessary and required.

**Common trap:** The complex phrasing with 'obviate the necessity' creates a double-negative situation that can cause confusion. Breaking down the vocabulary is critical:
- Question essentially asks: 'Can we skip bracing?'
- Answer: 'No, we cannot skip bracing.'

**Step 6: Beam stability factor**
Since bracing is required, must calculate $C_L$:

$R_B = \sqrt{\frac{\ell_u d}{b^2}} = \sqrt{\frac{108 \times 11.25}{3.5^2}} = \sqrt{\frac{1215}{12.25}} = \sqrt{99.2} = 9.96$

This requires beam stability factor reduction in design.

**Answer: NO, the unbraced length (ℓu/d = 9.6 > 7) does NOT obviate (eliminate) the necessity for lateral bracing. Bracing IS REQUIRED.**
