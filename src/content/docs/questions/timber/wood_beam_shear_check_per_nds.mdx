<TabItem label='Question'>
:::caution[Difficulty: **Medium** - Category: **Timber**]
A **2×10** Southern Pine beam (actual size 1.5 in × 9.25 in) is simply supported over 12 ft and carries a uniform load of $800~\text{lb/ft}$. Determine if the beam is adequate for **shear** assuming $F_v' = 135~\text{psi}$.
:::
</TabItem>

<TabItem label='Solution'>
:::tip[Solution]
**Step 1. Compute maximum shear:**

$$
V_{max} = \frac{wL}{2} = \frac{800(12)}{2} = 4800~\text{lb}
$$


**Step 2. Shear stress:**

$$
\tau = \frac{1.5V}{b d} = \frac{1.5(4800)}{1.5(9.25)} = 518~\text{psi}
$$

(Wait! That’s too high — convert properly)
Actually:

$$
\tau = \frac{1.5V}{b d} = \frac{1.5(4800)}{1.5(9.25)} \times \frac{1}{(1.5)(9.25)}
$$

Let’s compute correctly:

$$
A = b d = 1.5 \times 9.25 = 13.875~\text{in}^2
$$


$$
\tau = \frac{1.5V}{A} = \frac{1.5(4800)}{13.875} = 519~\text{psi}
$$

**Compare:** $519 > 135~\text{psi}$ → **Beam fails in shear.**
Thus, larger section or reduced load required.
:::
</TabItem>
