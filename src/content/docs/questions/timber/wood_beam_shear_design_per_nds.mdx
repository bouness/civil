<TabItem label='Question'>
:::caution[Difficulty: **Medium** - Category: **Timber**]
A sawn lumber beam (No. 2 Douglas Fir-Larch) is $3.5~\text{in}$ wide and $9.25~\text{in}$ deep, spanning $12~\text{ft}$ simply supported, carrying a uniformly distributed load of $400~\text{lb/ft}$. The allowable shear stress $F_v' = 180~\text{psi}$. Check adequacy in shear.
:::
</TabItem>

<TabItem label='Solution'>
:::tip[Solution]
Maximum shear:

$$
V_{max} = \frac{wL}{2} = \frac{400(12)}{2} = 2400~\text{lb}
$$

Beam area for shear:

$$
A = b \times d = 3.5 \times 9.25 = 32.4~\text{in}^2
$$

Nominal shear stress:

$$
v = \frac{V_{max}}{A} = \frac{2400}{32.4} = 74.1~\text{psi}
$$

Compare to allowable:

$$
74.1 < 180~\text{psi}
$$

Thus, the beam is **adequate in shear**.
:::
</TabItem>
