<TabItem label='Question'>
:::caution[Difficulty: **Medium** - Category: **Beams**]
A W14x90 beam ($b_f/2t_f = 10.2$, $h/t_w = 25.9$) of A992 steel is used as a simply supported beam. Determine if the section is compact, noncompact, or slender for flexure.
:::
</TabItem>

<TabItem label='Solution'>
:::tip[Solution]
**Local Buckling Classification**

**Step 1: Flange compactness limit**
For A992 steel ($F_y = 50~\text{ksi}$):
$\lambda_p = 0.38\sqrt{\frac{E}{F_y}} = 0.38\sqrt{\frac{29,000}{50}} = 0.38\times24.08 = 9.15$

Given $b_f/2t_f = 10.2 > 9.15$

**Therefore, flanges are NONCOMPACT**

**Step 2: Web compactness limit**
$\lambda_p = 3.76\sqrt{\frac{E}{F_y}} = 3.76\times24.08 = 90.5$

Given $h/t_w = 25.9 < 90.5$

**Therefore, web is COMPACT**

**Step 3: Overall section classification**
Since the flanges are noncompact, the entire section is classified as **NONCOMPACT**.

**Step 4: Implications for design**
For noncompact sections:
- The section cannot develop full plastic moment capacity $M_p$
- Local buckling may limit the moment capacity
- Use AISC Equation F3-1 for flexural strength

**Section classification: NONCOMPACT**
:::
