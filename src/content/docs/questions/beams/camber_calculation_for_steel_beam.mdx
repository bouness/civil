<TabItem label='Question'>
:::caution[Difficulty: **Medium** - Category: **Beams**]
A W24x76 beam ($I_x = 2100~\text{in}^4$) spans 40 ft and carries a total service dead load of 1.8 kips/ft. Determine the recommended camber for the beam. ($E = 29,000~\text{ksi}$)
:::
</TabItem>

<TabItem label='Solution'>
:::tip[Solution]
**Beam Camber Calculation**

**Step 1: Calculate dead load deflection**
$w_D = 1.8~\text{kips/ft} = 150~\text{lb/in}$

$\Delta_D = \frac{5wL^4}{384EI} = \frac{5 \times 150 \times (480)^4}{384 \times 29,000,000 \times 2100}$

Calculate numerator: $(480)^4 = 53.0\times10^9$
$5 \times 150 \times 53.0\times10^9 = 39.75\times10^{12}$

Calculate denominator: $384 \times 29\times10^6 \times 2100 = 384 \times 60.9\times10^9 = 23.4\times10^{12}$

$\Delta_D = \frac{39.75\times10^{12}}{23.4\times10^{12}} = 1.70~\text{in}$

**Step 2: Determine recommended camber**
Common practice is to camber for a portion of the dead load deflection:
- For beams: camber = $\frac{3}{4}$ to $\frac{5}{6}$ of dead load deflection
- For girders: camber = $\frac{1}{2}$ to $\frac{3}{4}$ of dead load deflection

For this beam, use $\frac{3}{4}$ of dead load deflection:

Camber = $0.75 \times 1.70 = 1.28~\text{in}$

**Step 3: Round to practical dimension**
Use $1\frac{1}{4}$-in camber

**Recommended camber: $1\frac{1}{4}$ inches**
