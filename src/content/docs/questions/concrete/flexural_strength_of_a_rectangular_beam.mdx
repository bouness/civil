<TabItem label='Question'>
:::caution[Difficulty: **Medium** - Category: **Concrete**]
A simply supported rectangular beam is reinforced with tension steel only. The beam has a width $b = 12~\text{in}$, effective depth $d = 20~\text{in}$, and contains four #8 bars ($A_s = 4 \times 0.79 = 3.16~\text{in}^2$). The concrete compressive strength is $f'_c = 4000~\text{psi}$ and the steel yield strength is $f_y = 60,000~\text{psi}$. Determine the nominal moment strength $M_n$ and design strength $\phi M_n$ assuming a tension-controlled section.
:::
</TabItem>

<TabItem label='Solution'>
:::tip[Solution]
**Given:** $b = 12~\text{in}$, $d = 20~\text{in}$, $A_s = 3.16~\text{in}^2$, $f'_c = 4000~\text{psi}$, $f_y = 60,000~\text{psi}$.

**Step 1.** Compute neutral axis depth $a$:

$$
a = \frac{A_s f_y}{0.85 f'_c b} = \frac{3.16(60,000)}{0.85(4000)(12)} = 4.65~\text{in}
$$


**Step 2.** Nominal moment strength:

$$
M_n = A_s f_y \left( d - \frac{a}{2} \right) = 3.16(60,000)(20 - 2.33) = 3.16(60,000)(17.67) = 3.35 \times 10^6~\text{lb-in}
$$


$$
M_n = \frac{3.35 \times 10^6}{12} = 279~\text{kip-ft}
$$


**Step 3.** Design strength for tension-controlled section ($\phi = 0.9$):

$$
\phi M_n = 0.9(279) = 251~\text{kip-ft}
$$


**Answer:** $M_n = 279~\text{kip-ft}$, $\phi M_n = 251~\text{kip-ft}$.
:::
