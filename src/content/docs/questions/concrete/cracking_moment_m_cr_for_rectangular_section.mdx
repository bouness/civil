<TabItem label='Question'>
:::caution[Difficulty: **Medium** - Category: **Concrete**]
Estimate the cracking moment $M_{cr}$ for a rectangular 12 in × 6 in concrete strip (b × h), with $f'_c = 4000~\text{psi}$. Use modulus of rupture approximation 
$$
f_r = 7.5\sqrt{f'_c}
$$
 (psi) and section modulus about extreme fiber 
$$
S=\dfrac{b h^2}{6}
$$
. Give $M_{cr}$ in kip-ft.
:::
</TabItem>

<TabItem label='Solution'>
:::tip[Solution]
Compute $\sqrt{f'_c}=63.2456$.  

$$
f_r=7.5(63.2456)=474.34~\text{psi}.
$$
  
Section modulus:

$$
S=\frac{12(6^2)}{6}=\frac{12\times36}{6}=72~\text{in}^3.
$$
  
Cracking moment (in-lb):

$$
M_{cr}=f_r S = 474.34\times72 = 34,157~\text{in-lb}.
$$
  
Convert to kip-ft: divide by 12,000:  

$$
M_{cr}=\frac{34,157}{12,000}=2.846~\text{kip-ft}.
$$
  

**Answer:** $M_{cr}\approx2.85~\text{kip-ft}$. 
:::
</TabItem>
