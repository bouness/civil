<TabItem label='Question'>
:::danger[Difficulty: **Hard** - Category: **Concrete**]
A column 12 ft tall is pinned at both ends and carries an axial load of $P_u = 200~\text{kips}$. The factored end moments are $M_1 = 10~\text{kip-ft}$ and $M_2 = 30~\text{kip-ft}$. The column is $12 \times 18~\text{in}$ with $f'_c = 4000~\text{psi}$, $f_y = 60,000~\text{psi}$, and reinforcement ratio $\rho = 1.5\%$. Determine the moment magnification factor $\delta$ using ACI 318 approximate method (non-sway).
:::
</TabItem>

<TabItem label='Solution'>
:::tip[Solution]
**Step 1.** Compute effective length and slenderness ratio:

$$
kL_u/r = \frac{1.0(12 \times 12)}{r}
$$

For rectangular section, $r = 0.3h = 0.3(18) = 5.4~\text{in}$.

$$
kL_u/r = \frac{144}{5.4} = 26.7 < 34
$$


Since $kL_u/r < 34$, slenderness effects are small, but ACI allows approximate magnification:

$$
\delta = \frac{1}{1 - P_u/P_{cr}}
$$


**Step 2.** Compute $P_{cr}$:

$$
P_{cr} = \frac{\pi^2 E I_g}{(kL_u)^2}
$$

Take $E = 57,000\sqrt{f'_c} = 57,000(63.25) = 3.6 \times 10^6~\text{psi}$.

$$
I_g = \frac{b h^3}{12} = \frac{12(18^3)}{12} = 5832~\text{in}^4
$$


$$
P_{cr} = \frac{\pi^2 (3.6 \times 10^6)(5832)}{144^2} = 3.2 \times 10^6~\text{lb} = 3200~\text{kips}
$$


**Step 3.** Compute $\delta$:

$$
\delta = \frac{1}{1 - (200/3200)} = 1.07
$$


**Answer:** $\delta = 1.07$ â€” small magnification; column is effectively short.
:::
