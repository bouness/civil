<TabItem label='Question'>
:::note[Difficulty: **Easy** - Category: **Concrete**]
If a concrete slab undergoes a free shrinkage strain of $\varepsilon_{sh} = 500~\mu\varepsilon$ (microstrain), estimate the linear shortening (in inches) of a 10 ft long slab strip. (Recall $1~\mu\varepsilon = 10^{-6}$.)
:::
</TabItem>

<TabItem label='Solution'>
:::tip[Solution]
Convert strain: $\varepsilon_{sh}=500\times10^{-6}=0.0005$.  
Length $L=10~\text{ft}=120~\text{in}$.  
Linear shortening $\Delta = \varepsilon_{sh} L = 0.0005(120)=0.06~\text{in}$.  

**Answer:** Expected free shrinkage shortening $=0.06~\text{in}$. 
:::
</TabItem>
