<TabItem label='Question'>
:::note[Difficulty: **Easy** - Category: **Concrete**]
A 12-inch-wide, 24-inch-deep concrete beam uses $f_y = 60,000~\text{psi}$ steel and $f'_c = 4000~\text{psi}$. Determine the minimum required steel area $A_{s,\text{min}}$ for flexure per ACI 318.
:::
</TabItem>

<TabItem label='Solution'>
:::tip[Solution]
**Formula (ACI 318-14, Eq. 9.6.1.2):**

$$
A_{s,\text{min}} = \max \left( 3\sqrt{f'_c} \frac{b d}{f_y}, \frac{200 b d}{f_y} \right)
$$

Given $b = 12~\text{in}$, $d = 24~\text{in}$, $f'_c = 4000~\text{psi}$, $f_y = 60,000~\text{psi}$.

Compute both terms:

$$
A_{s1} = 3\sqrt{4000}\frac{12(24)}{60,000} = 3(63.25)(288)/60,000 = 0.91~\text{in}^2
$$


$$
A_{s2} = \frac{200(12)(24)}{60,000} = 0.96~\text{in}^2
$$


**Answer:** $A_{s,\text{min}} = 0.96~\text{in}^2$
:::
</TabItem>
