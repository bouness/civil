<TabItem label='Question'>
:::danger[Difficulty: **Hard** - Category: **Concrete**]
A short column has gross area $A_g = 400~\text{in}^2$, nominal axial strength (concrete only) $0.85f'_cA_g = 0.85(4,000)(400)$ in-lb units (conceptual). Compute the nominal axial capacity $P_n$ in kips ignoring steel contribution.
:::
</TabItem>

<TabItem label='Solution'>
:::tip[Solution]
**Compute $0.85f'_cA_g$:**
First compute $f'_c A_g = 4,000 \times 400 = 1,600,000~\text{psi·in}^2$ (units consistent produce lb).
Multiply by 0.85:

$$
P_n = 0.85(1,600,000) = 1,360,000~\text{lb}
$$

Convert to kips:

$$
P_n = \frac{1,360,000}{1000} = 1,360~\text{kips}
$$

Thus, **nominal axial capacity (concrete only) = 1,360 kips** (steel ignored; in practice include reinforcement and eccentricity).
:::
</TabItem>
