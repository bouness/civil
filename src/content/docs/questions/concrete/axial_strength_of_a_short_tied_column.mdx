<TabItem label='Question'>
:::caution[Difficulty: **Medium** - Category: **Concrete**]
A short tied column has a cross-section of $16 \times 16~\text{in}$ with eight #8 bars ($A_s = 8 \times 0.79 = 6.32~\text{in}^2$). The concrete strength is $f'_c = 4000~\text{psi}$ and steel yield strength is $f_y = 60,000~\text{psi}$. Determine the nominal and design axial strength per ACI 318 for a tied column.
:::
</TabItem>

<TabItem label='Solution'>
:::tip[Solution]
**Step 1.** Nominal axial strength for a tied column:

$$
P_n = 0.85 f'_c (A_g - A_s) + f_y A_s
$$

Given $A_g = 16 \times 16 = 256~\text{in}^2$.

$$
P_n = 0.85(4000)(256 - 6.32) + 60,000(6.32)
$$


$$
P_n = 0.85(4000)(249.68) + 379,200 = 849,000 + 379,200 = 1.23 \times 10^6~\text{lb} = 1230~\text{kips}
$$


**Step 2.** Design axial strength:
For tied columns, $\phi = 0.65$:

$$
\phi P_n = 0.65(1230) = 800~\text{kips}
$$


**Answer:** $P_n = 1230~\text{kips}$, $\phi P_n = 800~\text{kips}$.
:::
</TabItem>
