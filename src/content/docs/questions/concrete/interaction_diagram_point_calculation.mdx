<TabItem label='Question'>
:::danger[Difficulty: **Hard** - Category: **Concrete**]
For a rectangular column section 14 Ã— 20 in with eight #8 bars equally distributed, $f'_c = 4000~\text{psi}$ and $f_y = 60,000~\text{psi}$, compute the nominal axial load $P_n$ and moment $M_n$ when the steel strain just reaches yield in tension ($\epsilon_t = 0.002$).
:::
</TabItem>

<TabItem label='Solution'>
:::tip[Solution]
**Step 1.** Assume a linear strain distribution with $\epsilon_c = 0.003$ at the compression fiber.
From strain compatibility:

$$
\frac{c - d_t}{c} = \frac{\epsilon_t}{\epsilon_c} = \frac{0.002}{0.003} = 0.667 \Rightarrow d_t = 0.333c
$$

Assume $d_t = 18.5~\text{in}$ (top to tension steel), so:

$$
c = \frac{18.5}{0.333} = 55.6~\text{in}
$$


**Step 2.** Calculate equivalent stress block depth:

$$
a = \beta_1 c = 0.85(55.6) = 47.3~\text{in}
$$


$$
A_s = 8(0.79) = 6.32~\text{in}^2,\quad A_g = 14(20) = 280~\text{in}^2
$$


**Step 3.** Nominal axial strength:

$$
P_n = 0.85f'_c(b a) + A_s f_y (tension)
$$


$$
P_n = 0.85(4000)(14)(47.3) - 6.32(60,000)
$$


$$
P_n = 2.26 \times 10^6 - 379,200 = 1.88 \times 10^6~\text{lb} = 1880~\text{kips}
$$


**Step 4.** Nominal moment:

$$
M_n = A_s f_y (d - a/2) = 6.32(60,000)(18.5 - 23.65)
$$


$$
M_n = -1.96 \times 10^6~\text{lb-in} = 163~\text{kip-ft}
$$


**Answer:** $P_n = 1880~\text{kips}$, $M_n = 163~\text{kip-ft}$.
:::
