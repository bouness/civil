<TabItem label='Question'>
:::danger[Difficulty: **Hard** - Category: **Concrete**]
A simply supported concrete beam (normal weight) has $b = 12~\text{in}$, $d = 20~\text{in}$, $f'_c = 4000~\text{psi}$, and $f_y = 60,000~\text{psi}$. The beam contains #3 stirrups spaced at $s = 8~\text{in}$ with two legs.
Determine the **nominal shear strength $V_n$** using ACI 318 provisions.
:::
</TabItem>

<TabItem label='Solution'>
:::tip[Solution]
**Step 1. Compute concrete shear strength:**

$$
V_c = 2\sqrt{f'_c} b d / 1000
$$


$$
V_c = 2(\sqrt{4000})(12)(20)/1000 = 2(63.25)(240)/1000 = 30.36~\text{kips}
$$


**Step 2. Compute stirrup shear strength:**

$$
A_v = 2(0.11) = 0.22~\text{in}^2
$$


$$
V_s = \frac{A_v f_y d}{s}\frac{1}{1000} = \frac{0.22(60,000)(20)}{8 \times 1000} = 33.0~\text{kips}
$$


**Step 3. Total nominal shear strength:**

$$
V_n = V_c + V_s = 30.4 + 33.0 = 63.4~\text{kips}
$$

:::
</TabItem>
