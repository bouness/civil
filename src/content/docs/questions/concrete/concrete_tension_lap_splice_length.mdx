<TabItem label='Question'>
:::caution[Difficulty: **Medium** - Category: **Concrete**]
Two #5 bars are lap spliced in tension in $f'_c = 4000~\text{psi}$ concrete. Using ACI-type empirical formula $l_s = 0.08 f_y d_b / \sqrt{f'_c}$ with $f_y = 60~\text{ksi}$ and $d_b = 0.625~\text{in}$ (#5), compute $l_s$ (in inches).
:::
</TabItem>

<TabItem label='Solution'>
:::tip[Solution]
**Compute $\sqrt{f'_c}$:**

$$
\sqrt{4000} = 63.2455532...
$$

Now compute numerator:

$$
0.08 f_y d_b = 0.08(60,000)(0.625)
$$

Calculate step-by-step:
$60,000 \times 0.625 = 37,500$. Then $0.08 \times 37,500 = 3,000$.
Divide by $\sqrt{f'_c}$:

$$
l_s = \frac{3000}{63.2455532} = 47.4341649...~\text{in}
$$

Round: **$l_s \approx 47.4~\text{in}$** (approx 4.0 ft).
:::
</TabItem>
