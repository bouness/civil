<TabItem label='Question'>
:::caution[Difficulty: **Medium** - Category: **Concrete**]
A simply supported concrete beam (normal weight) with $b = 12~\text{in}$ and $d = 18~\text{in}$ carries a factored shear $V_u = 25~\text{kips}$. Concrete strength $f'_c = 4000~\text{psi}$. No shear reinforcement is provided. Check if concrete shear strength $\phi V_c$ is adequate ($\phi = 0.75$).
:::
</TabItem>

<TabItem label='Solution'>
:::tip[Solution]
Per ACI 318:

$$
V_c = 2\sqrt{f'_c} b d / 1000 = 2\sqrt{4000}(12)(18)/1000 = 2(63.25)(216)/1000 = 27.3~\text{kips}
$$

Design strength:

$$
\phi V_c = 0.75(27.3) = 20.5~\text{kips}
$$

Compare:

$$
V_u = 25~\text{kips} > 20.5~\text{kips}
$$

â†’ Beam **requires shear reinforcement**.
:::
</TabItem>
